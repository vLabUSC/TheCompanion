# CLAUDE.md — Situated Player Roles

Four roles that define the design lens for each course assignment. The published overview is `Situated Player Roles.md` — read it for the framework's intent and the discovery-vs-control axis.

## The four roles

| File | Role | Central question | Assignment lens |
|---|---|---|---|
| `SPR 1 - The Investigator, World as Evidence.md` | Investigator | What happened? | World as Evidence |
| `SPR 2 - The Traveler, World as Passage.md` | Traveler | What am I experiencing? | World as Passage |
| `SPR 3 - The Entrant, World as Threshold.md` | Entrant | How does this world work? | World as Threshold |
| `SPR 4 - The Dreamer, World as Enigma.md` | Dreamer | What does this world express? | World as Enigma |

Investigator + Traveler emphasize discovery; Entrant + Dreamer emphasize control. Dreamer builds on the Entrant foundation — same mechanics, different meaning.

Notation rule: when "Entrant" appears, "Dreamer" follows it adjacently — *"Entrant, Dreamer"*.
