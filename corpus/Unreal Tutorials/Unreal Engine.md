Unreal Engine is a real-time 3D creation platform developed by Epic Games. It is one of the two dominant game engines in the industry, alongside Unity, and is the primary tool used in this course.

## History

Epic Games released the first Unreal Engine in 1998, alongside the first-person shooter *Unreal*. For years it was primarily a tool for high-end action games. Over successive versions, Epic expanded its scope: Unreal Engine 4 (2014) opened the platform to independent developers with a free licensing model; Unreal Engine 5 (2022) introduced Nanite (virtualized geometry) and Lumen (dynamic global illumination), making film-quality real-time environments accessible to small teams.

Epic has positioned Unreal Engine beyond games — it is used in film and television production (virtual production stages), architecture visualization, live events, and simulation.
